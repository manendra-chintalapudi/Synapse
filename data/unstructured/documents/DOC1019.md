# RCA Report — DOC1019
**Document type:** Root Cause Analysis Report
**Related failure:** F1072  |  **RCA record:** RCA1072
**Equipment:** EQ-LF-01 — Ladle Furnace Line 1 (Ladle Furnace)
**Location:** Mumbai Unit - Bay 1
**Event timestamp:** 2026-04-01 01:34:37
**Failure mode:** Heat Dissipation Failure (source flag: HDF)

## 1. Incident Summary
On 2026-04-01 01:34:37, EQ-LF-01 (Ladle Furnace Line 1) experienced a heat dissipation failure that
interrupted normal operation. The condition-monitoring system captured the following process values at the time of
the event: air temperature 298.5 K, process temperature 310.3 K,
rotational speed 2572 rpm, torque 56.2 Nm, and accumulated tool wear
141 minutes. The event was logged by the equipment engineer on shift and escalated for formal
root cause analysis under the plant's CMMS reliability programme.

## 2. Investigation & Findings
A cross-functional review examined operating logs, the sensor trace immediately preceding the trip, recent maintenance
history and the applicable operating procedure for the ladle furnace. Trends in the minutes before the event were
compared against the equipment's qualified operating envelope. The investigation focused on whether the observed values
represented a developing fault or a transient excursion, and whether any preventive-maintenance trigger had been missed.

## 3. Root Cause
Root cause traced to insufficient heat dissipation: cooling airflow and coolant flow were below the required rate while process temperature rose relative to ambient, exceeding the tool's thermal limit. Contributing factors included clogged cooling passages and degraded coolant.

## 4. Corrective Action
Cleaned and restored cooling circuit, replaced coolant, and added a temperature-differential alarm to the monitoring system.

## 5. Preventive Recommendations
To reduce recurrence risk, the team recommends reinforcing the condition-based maintenance thresholds associated with
this failure mode, updating the relevant operating procedure, and adding the event to the equipment's reliability
history for trend analysis. Spare-part availability for the affected sub-assembly should be confirmed, and operators
briefed on the early-warning indicators identified during this investigation.
