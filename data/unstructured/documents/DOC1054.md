# Standard Operating Procedure — DOC1054
**Title:** Ladle Furnace Emergency Response Procedure
**Procedure ID:** PROC-021  |  **Applies to:** EQ-LF-02 — Ladle Furnace Line 2 (Ladle Furnace)
**Location:** Mumbai Unit - Bay 1

## 1. Purpose
This procedure defines the standard method for the task titled "Ladle Furnace Emergency Response Procedure" on the ladle furnace. It exists
to ensure the activity is carried out safely, consistently and in a way that protects personnel, equipment and product
quality, and to provide a training and audit reference for operators and maintenance staff.

## 2. Scope
This SOP applies to all operators, maintenance technicians and supervisors who operate or work on EQ-LF-02
(Ladle Furnace Line 2) at Mumbai Unit - Bay 1. It covers the normal execution of the task and the
associated safety controls. It does not replace equipment-specific manufacturer instructions or statutory requirements.

## 3. Responsibilities
The shift supervisor authorises the activity and confirms isolation where required. The assigned technician executes the
steps and records completion. The equipment engineer maintains this procedure and reviews it after any related incident.

## 4. Prerequisites & PPE
Confirm the correct permit-to-work is in place, required isolations are applied, and standard PPE plus any
task-specific protection (as noted below) is worn. Ensure tools, spares and measuring equipment are available and
calibrated before starting.

## 5. Procedure Steps
1. On detection of an abnormal condition on the ladle furnace, actuate the nearest emergency stop and evacuate personnel from the hazard zone.
2. Notify the control room and shift supervisor immediately.
3. Isolate energy sources and, if safe, contain any spill or leak.
4. Do not restart until a root-cause check and safety clearance are complete.

## 6. Safety Notes
- Arc flash and intense UV - approved shielding and PPE required.
- Molten steel and slag - exclusion zone during heating and stirring.
- High-voltage electrical system - isolate and earth before any work.
- Stop work and escalate to the shift supervisor if any parameter falls outside the normal operating band
  (reference: Arc power: 8-25 MW, Heating rate: 3-5 degC/min).
