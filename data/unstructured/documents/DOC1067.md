# Equipment Manual — DOC1067
**Equipment:** EQ-CNV-01 — Raw Material Conveyor #1
**Type:** Conveyor System  |  **Location:** Rajendra Steel Plant, Mumbai Unit - Raw Material Yard  |  **Commissioned:** 2015-11-08
**Preventive-maintenance interval:** monthly

## 1. Overview
The Raw Material Conveyor #1 (EQ-CNV-01) is the belt conveyor transporting raw materials (ore, coke, flux) across the handling yard. It is installed at Rajendra Steel Plant, Mumbai Unit - Raw Material Yard and was
commissioned on 2015-11-08. It is a critical asset in the plant's production flow and is managed under the
CMMS reliability programme, with a formal preventive-maintenance interval of **monthly**. This manual excerpt
summarises the machine's key specifications, normal operating envelope, common failure modes and maintenance and safety
requirements. It should be read together with the original equipment manufacturer (OEM) documentation and the relevant
standard operating procedures; the values quoted here are indicative, industry-typical figures for a machine of this
class rather than exact OEM design data.

## 2. Key Specifications
- **conveyor type**: belt conveyor
- **capacity tph**: 800
- **belt width mm**: 1400
- **belt speed m per s**: 2.5
- **material**: iron ore / coke / flux

## 3. Operating Parameters
The equipment must be operated within its qualified envelope; representative set-points include conveyor type = belt conveyor, capacity tph = 800, belt width mm = 1400. Operators
log the principal parameters each shift and compare them against these limits. Any sustained excursion outside the
normal band — particularly on temperature, pressure, tension, cooling-water flow or drive load as applicable to this
machine — must be raised through the CMMS and, where product or safety is affected, escalated for root cause analysis.
Start-up and shutdown follow the dedicated SOP, bringing the machine to condition gradually while watching for abnormal
vibration, noise or thermal signatures.

## 4. Common Failure Modes
The following failure modes are characteristic of this machine type and drive its inspection and spares strategy:
- **belt mistracking**
- **belt tear / wear**
- **idler / roller seizure**
- **drive motor failure**
- **material carryback / spillage**

Early-warning indicators for these modes (temperature drift, abnormal vibration, tension/level instability, water-flow
loss) are trended in the condition-monitoring system so that developing faults are caught before they cause an
unplanned outage or a quality deviation on the product.

## 5. Maintenance Schedule
| Frequency | Task |
|-----------|------|

| Monthly (primary PM) | Full preventive-maintenance service per the CMMS schedule for this asset type, including wear-part inspection and calibration. |
| Annual / campaign | Major inspection, structural/refractory assessment and overhaul of critical sub-assemblies. |

All maintenance is recorded against EQ-CNV-01 in the CMMS. Findings that affect product or safety are raised
as work orders and, where warranted, as formal deviations.

## 6. Safety Warnings
- Moving belt, pulleys and idlers — guards fitted and lockout-tagout before clearing jams.
- Nip points at drive and tail pulleys — never clean or adjust a running conveyor.
- Airborne dust and spillage — respiratory PPE and housekeeping to prevent fire/slip hazards.
- Only trained, authorised personnel may operate or maintain this equipment, under the plant permit-to-work system and
  in compliance with The Factories Act, 1948 safety provisions.
