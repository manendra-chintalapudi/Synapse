The root cause analysis RCA1010, performed by Sunita Rao (maintenance technician, Shift A), concluded: On the Runout Table Cooling System, strip coiling temperature crept above target because a bank of laminar headers delivered reduced flow — nozzle scaling and a sticking flow-control valve cut water to the top headers, so the strip was under-cooled across part of the table.

## 3. Procedure Review
The investigation reviewed the governing procedure PROC-002 for this asset and recorded a compliance finding: Step 1 of PROC-002 ("Allow the cooling bed to cool to a safe inspection temperature and confirm atmosphere is safe to enter or approach.") — cooling/lubrication verification was not completed at the required interval before the event. This finding is the actionable link between the maintenance routine and the failure mechanism, and it was logged so recurrence checks can compare like-for-like across the fleet.

## 4. Corrective Work Completed
Cleared the scaled header nozzles, overhauled the flow-control valve, and added header-bank flow monitoring tied to the coiling-temperature model so an under-delivering bank is detected immediately. Post-repair verification confirmed return to the qualified operating envelope before release to production.
