## 1. Incident Narrative
At 19:40:09 on 2026-05-14, the Ladle Furnace #1 raised a heat dissipation failure alarm and was taken out of normal operation. Condition monitoring captured air temperature 302.5 K, process temperature 311.6 K, rotational speed 1917 rpm, torque 65.5 Nm and accumulated tool wear 108 minutes at the moment of the trip. The shift crew isolated the equipment under the plant permit-to-work system and raised this work order for investigation and repair.

## 2. Response & Diagnosis
Ravi Singh (Shift C), Neha Menon (Shift C) attended the equipment and completed the make-safe checks before handover to the investigation team. The root cause analysis RCA1015, performed by Suresh Malhotra (equipment engineer, Shift C), concluded: Ladle Furnace #1's water-cooled roof panel ran above its rated temperature during an extended hold: coolant flow drifted low relative to arc load while scale in the roof cooling passages cut heat extraction. Degraded coolant compounded the loss, so the panel could not shed the arc's radiant heat.

## 3. Procedure Review
The investigation reviewed the governing procedure PROC-004 for this asset and recorded a compliance finding: Step 1 of PROC-004 ("Allow the ladle furnace to cool to a safe inspection temperature and confirm atmosphere is safe to enter or approach.") — cooling/lubrication verification was not completed at the required interval before the event. This finding is the actionable link between the maintenance routine and the failure mechanism, and it was logged so recurrence checks can compare like-for-like across the fleet.

## 4. Corrective Work Completed
Descaled and recharged the roof-panel cooling circuit, replaced the coolant, and interlocked extended holds against measured cooling-water flow so a hold cannot outrun available cooling. Post-repair verification confirmed return to the qualified operating envelope before release to production.
