## 1. Incident Narrative
At 00:22:18 on 2025-09-09, the Overhead Crane (Rolling Mill Bay) #1 raised a power failure alarm and was taken out of normal operation. Condition monitoring captured air temperature 303.0 K, process temperature 312.2 K, rotational speed 1226 rpm, torque 74.8 Nm and accumulated tool wear 67 minutes at the moment of the trip. The shift crew isolated the equipment under the plant permit-to-work system and raised this work order for investigation and repair.

## 2. Response & Diagnosis
Karthik Chatterjee (Shift A), Swati Desai (Shift A) attended the equipment and completed the make-safe checks before handover to the investigation team. The root cause analysis RCA1016, performed by Ravi Singh (equipment engineer, Shift C), concluded: Overhead Crane (Rolling Mill Bay) #1 tripped its travel/hoist drive on overcurrent: a stalled motion under load drew fault current, indicating an electrical/drive fault rather than structural overload. A worn contactor and marginal motor insulation contributed.

## 3. Procedure Review
The investigation reviewed the governing procedure PROC-029 for this asset and recorded a compliance finding: Step 3 of PROC-029 ("Verify cooling and hydraulic circuits are at specified flow and pressure, and confirm electrical isolation points and drive calibration against reference values.") — the electrical isolation/calibration check was missed in the prior PM cycle. This finding is the actionable link between the maintenance routine and the failure mechanism, and it was logged so recurrence checks can compare like-for-like across the fleet.

## 4. Corrective Work Completed
Replaced the affected drive components and contactor, megger-tested the motor windings, and added overcurrent-trend logging to the monthly electrical check. Parts and consumables used were booked to this work order, and post-repair verification confirmed the equipment returned to its qualified operating envelope before release back to production.
