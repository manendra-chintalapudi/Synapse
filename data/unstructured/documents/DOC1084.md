At 04:21:36 on 2026-01-15, the Electric Arc Furnace #1 raised a heat dissipation failure alarm and was taken out of normal operation. Condition monitoring captured air temperature 297.4 K, process temperature 307.9 K, rotational speed 2738 rpm, torque 37.9 Nm and accumulated tool wear 123 minutes at the moment of the trip. The shift crew isolated the equipment under the plant permit-to-work system and raised this work order for investigation and repair.

## 2. Response & Diagnosis
Shalini Naidu (Shift B) attended the equipment and completed the make-safe checks before handover to the investigation team. The root cause analysis RCA1017, performed by Shalini Naidu (equipment engineer, Shift B), concluded: Electric Arc Furnace #1 developed a refractory hot spot on the slag line: with an arc/O2 profile that ran hard, localized lining thinning let the shell temperature at that panel climb toward its limit. Slag chemistry drifting off-target accelerated chemical attack of the lining at the hot face.

## 3. Procedure Review
The investigation reviewed the governing procedure PROC-005 for this asset and recorded a compliance finding: Step 2 of PROC-005 ("Inspect bearings, seals, couplings and drive components for wear, and top up or replace lubricants as specified.") — cooling/lubrication verification was not completed at the required interval before the event. This finding is the actionable link between the maintenance routine and the failure mechanism, and it was logged so recurrence checks can compare like-for-like across the fleet.

## 4. Corrective Work Completed
Gunned and repaired the slag-line hot spot, corrected the slag-basicity practice, and added slag-line thermography to the shift checks so thinning is caught before any burn-through risk. Parts and consumables used were booked to this work order, and post-repair verification confirmed the equipment returned to its qualified operating envelope before release back to production.
