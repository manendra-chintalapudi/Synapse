## 1. Incident Narrative
At 07:41:12 on 2026-05-27, the Reheating Furnace #1 raised a heat dissipation failure alarm and was taken out of normal operation. Condition monitoring captured air temperature 301.7 K, process temperature 312.9 K, rotational speed 2447 rpm, torque 41.8 Nm and accumulated tool wear 188 minutes at the moment of the trip. The shift crew isolated the equipment under the plant permit-to-work system and raised this work order for investigation and repair.

## 2. Response & Diagnosis
Divya Banerjee (Shift B) attended the equipment and completed the make-safe checks before handover to the investigation team. The root cause analysis RCA1030, performed by Naveen Malhotra (equipment engineer, Shift C), concluded: Reheating Furnace #1 could not hold soak-zone temperature within band without over-firing, and roof/skid thermocouples showed rising heat loss — consistent with refractory wear opening the lining and skid-button wear letting billets contact the water-cooled skids. Degraded lining and worn buttons together raised parasitic heat loss and produced uneven billet heating.

## 3. Procedure Review
The investigation reviewed the governing procedure PROC-022 for this asset and recorded a compliance finding: Step 4 of PROC-022 ("Record final temperatures and any anomalies in the shift log.") — cooling/lubrication verification was not completed at the required interval before the event. This finding is the actionable link between the maintenance routine and the failure mechanism, and it was logged so recurrence checks can compare like-for-like across the fleet.

## 4. Corrective Work Completed
Patched the worn soak-zone refractory, replaced the skid buttons and re-tuned the zone firing curve; added a soak-zone heat-loss check to the quarterly refractory survey. Parts and consumables used were booked to this work order, and post-repair verification confirmed the equipment returned to its qualified operating envelope before release back to production.
