## 1. Incident Narrative
At 07:12:22 on 2025-09-14, the Overhead Crane (Rolling Mill Bay) #1 raised a power failure alarm and was taken out of normal operation. Condition monitoring captured air temperature 304.3 K, process temperature 314.8 K, rotational speed 1525 rpm, torque 68.9 Nm and accumulated tool wear 35 minutes at the moment of the trip. The shift crew isolated the equipment under the plant permit-to-work system and raised this work order for investigation and repair.

## 2. Response & Diagnosis
Karthik Chatterjee (Shift A), Swati Desai (Shift A) attended the equipment and completed the make-safe checks before handover to the investigation team. The root cause analysis RCA1060, performed by Shalini Naidu (equipment engineer, Shift B), concluded: Root cause was a power-delivery failure: the product of rotational speed and torque pushed process power outside the acceptable window, indicating a drive/supply anomaly. An unstable supply and drive fault were the primary contributors.

## 3. Procedure Review
The investigation reviewed the governing procedure PROC-029 for this asset and recorded a compliance finding: Step 3 of PROC-029 ("Verify cooling and hydraulic circuits are at specified flow and pressure, and confirm electrical isolation points and drive calibration against reference values.") — the electrical isolation/calibration check was missed in the prior PM cycle. This finding is the actionable link between the maintenance routine and the failure mechanism, and it was logged so recurrence checks can compare like-for-like across the fleet.

## 4. Corrective Work Completed
Inspected and repaired the drive, verified supply stability, and added power-envelope monitoring with an automatic trip. Parts and consumables used were booked to this work order, and post-repair verification confirmed the equipment returned to its qualified operating envelope before release back to production.
