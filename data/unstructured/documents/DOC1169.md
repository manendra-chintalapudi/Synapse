## 1. Incident Narrative
At 04:57:04 on 2026-06-02, the Continuous Casting Machine #1 raised a overstrain failure alarm and was taken out of normal operation. Condition monitoring captured air temperature 303.2 K, process temperature 311.7 K, rotational speed 1186 rpm, torque 12.5 Nm and accumulated tool wear 8 minutes at the moment of the trip. The shift crew isolated the equipment under the plant permit-to-work system and raised this work order for investigation and repair.

## 2. Response & Diagnosis
Anita Iyer (Shift A), Shalini Rao (Shift A) attended the equipment and completed the make-safe checks before handover to the investigation team. The root cause analysis RCA1109, performed by Naveen Malhotra (equipment engineer, Shift C), concluded: Continuous Casting Machine #1 tripped on segment overload: a withdrawal/strand-guide roll bound under load, driving segment forces above their limit during casting. Bearing wear and a misaligned roll concentrated stress on the segment, and the drive saw the reflected overload.

## 3. Procedure Review
The investigation reviewed the governing procedure PROC-009 for this asset and recorded a compliance finding: Step 3 of PROC-009 ("Isolate power and apply lockout-tagout, then bleed hydraulic and pneumatic pressure to zero.") — operating-limit verification was not performed prior to the event. This finding is the actionable link between the maintenance routine and the failure mechanism, and it was logged so recurrence checks can compare like-for-like across the fleet.

## 4. Corrective Work Completed
Replaced the seized roll and its bearings, re-aligned the affected segment, and added segment load-cell trending to catch a binding roll before it overloads the strand. Parts and consumables used were booked to this work order, and post-repair verification confirmed the equipment returned to its qualified operating envelope before release back to production. 

## 5. Closure
Work order closed on 2026-06-07 with the corrective action verified in service.
