- **Issuing body:** ASTM International
- **Title:** Standard Test Methods and Definitions for Mechanical Testing of Steel Products

## Scope (high level)

ASTM A370 defines the **test methods and terminology used for the mechanical testing of steel products** — wrought and cast carbon steels, stainless steels, and related alloys. Its purpose is to establish uniform procedures so that mechanical properties determined by different laboratories can be compared and used to evaluate a product's conformance to its material specification. The standard describes how tests are set up, how specimens are taken and oriented, and how results are reported and rounded — the exact specimen geometries, apparatus requirements, and calculation details are in the licensed full text and are **not** reproduced here.

## Test methods covered (publicly known)

ASTM A370 is an umbrella standard covering several categories of mechanical test:

- **Tension test** — for properties such as yield strength, tensile strength, elongation, and reduction of area
- **Bend test** — a ductility check via guided bending
- **Hardness testing** — including **Brinell** and **Rockwell** methods (and reference to portable hardness testing)
- **Charpy impact test** — notch-toughness assessment (Charpy V-notch)

It also includes definitions/terminology and, in its annexes, product-specific testing guidance (e.g., for bars, tubes, fasteners, and similar product forms).

## Purpose

To provide a single, internationally recognized reference for *how* to mechanically test steel products, so that material specifications can simply cite A370 rather than re-describe each test — supporting consistent quality evaluation across producers, fabricators, and inspection labs in construction, mechanical, automotive, and other industries.
