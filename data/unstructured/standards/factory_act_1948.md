# The Factories Act, 1948 (India) — Industrial Safety, Health, and Working Conditions

> **Scope / Summary Reference.** The Factories Act, 1948 is **Indian legislation and its full text is in the public domain**, freely available from official Government of India sources (see *Where to obtain the full text*). This document is a plain-language scope summary for RAG search context and paraphrases provisions rather than reproducing the full enacted text; it deliberately avoids quoting exact section wording or asserting specific numeric limits (thresholds, hours, penalties), which should be read from the authoritative text and current state amendments.

- **Instrument:** The Factories Act, 1948 (Act No. 63 of 1948), as amended
- **Issuing/enforcing authority:** Enacted by the Parliament of India; enforced primarily by **State Factory Inspectorates** (Chief Inspector of Factories) under the labour departments of each State/UT
- **Domain:** General industrial occupational safety, health, welfare, and conditions of work in factories

## Why this is the relevant safety framework for a steel plant

An integrated iron & steel works in India is a "factory" employing power and large numbers of workers, and it carries out processes listed as **hazardous** under the Act. This Act — together with the applicable **State Factory Rules** — is the core statutory safety-and-health framework governing a steel plant's workforce. (It is used here in place of OISD standards, which are scoped to the petroleum / oil-&-gas sector; see `oisd_relevant` note in the folder summary.)

## Applicability (high level)

The Act broadly applies to manufacturing premises using power with a workforce at or above a defined size, and to non-power premises above a higher defined size (the exact worker-count thresholds are set in the Act and may be tightened by State rules). It governs, among other things:

- **Health** — cleanliness, waste/effluent disposal, ventilation and temperature, dust and fume control, lighting, drinking water, sanitation.
- **Safety** — fencing/guarding of machinery, work on or near machinery, protection of eyes, precautions against fire, pressure-plant safety, hoists/lifts, and appointment of **Safety Officers** in larger works.
- **Welfare** — washing facilities, first-aid, canteens, rest rooms, crèches, and similar amenities.
- **Working conditions** — working hours, weekly holidays, overtime, leave, and restrictions on employment of young persons and women in certain operations.

## Hazardous processes (directly relevant to steel)

The Act contains special provisions for **"hazardous processes."** Its First Schedule of industries involving hazardous processes explicitly includes **ferrous metallurgical industries — such as integrated iron & steel, ferro-alloys, and special steels** — placing steel-plant operations squarely within the Act's most stringent safety obligations. These include enhanced duties around disclosure of hazards, worker participation in safety management, medical surveillance, emergency planning, and handling of dangerous materials.

## Safety Officer requirement

The Act requires the appointment of qualified **Safety Officers** in factories above a defined size, and additional safety-management obligations apply to factories carrying out hazardous processes (as a steel plant does). Exact triggers and duties are defined in the Act and the relevant State Factory Rules.

## Purpose

To secure the health, safety, and welfare of workers in factories; to regulate working conditions; and to place special controls on hazardous industrial processes so as to prevent injury, occupational disease, and major industrial accidents.
