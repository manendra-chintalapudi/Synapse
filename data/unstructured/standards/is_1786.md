- **Standard number:** IS 1786 (current edition commonly referenced: IS 1786:2008, with subsequent amendments)
- **Issuing body:** Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS), India
- **Title:** High Strength Deformed Steel Bars and Wires for Concrete Reinforcement — Specification

## Scope (high level)

IS 1786 specifies requirements for **high-strength deformed steel bars and wires used as reinforcement in concrete**. It is one of the primary Indian material specifications a producer of reinforcement (rebar) steel must conform to. At a high level the standard addresses the manufacture, chemical composition, mechanical properties, deformation/rib requirements for bond with concrete, testing, and marking of reinforcing bars — the specific numeric requirements for each of these are given in the licensed full text and are **not** reproduced here.

## Grade designations (publicly known names)

The standard defines reinforcement in a set of strength grades. The grade names are public classification labels (the numeric part broadly corresponds to a specified yield-strength level in MPa; a trailing **"D"** denotes a variant with enhanced ductility requirements):

- **Fe 415**, **Fe 415D**
- **Fe 500**, **Fe 500D**
- **Fe 550**, **Fe 550D**
- **Fe 600**

> Note: only the grade *names* are listed here. The associated yield-strength, tensile-strength, elongation, and bend/re-bend acceptance values are part of the copyrighted standard and are not restated in this reference.

## Purpose

To provide a common, testable material specification so that reinforcing steel supplied by different producers has consistent, verifiable strength, ductility, weldability, and bond characteristics — enabling safe and predictable design of reinforced-concrete structures.

## Governing committee

Developed and maintained under the BIS **Civil Engineering Division**, by the **Concrete Reinforcement Sectional Committee, CED 54**.
