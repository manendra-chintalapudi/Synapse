- **Standard number:** IS 2062 (current edition commonly referenced: IS 2062:2011, with amendments)
- **Issuing body:** Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS), India
- **Title:** Hot Rolled Medium and High Tensile Structural Steel — Specification

## Scope (high level)

IS 2062 specifies requirements for **hot-rolled structural steel** supplied as plates, strips, sections/shapes (beams, channels, angles), flats, and bars for general structural use — for example in buildings, bridges, industrial sheds, and heavy fabrication. At a high level it covers manufacture, chemical composition, mechanical properties, and testing for the various grades and sub-qualities. The specific numeric limits are given in the licensed full text and are **not** reproduced here.

## Grade designations (publicly known names)

The standard organizes structural steel into a set of strength grades (the numeric part broadly corresponds to a specified yield-strength level in MPa). Publicly documented grade names include:

- **E250**
- **E300**
- **E350**
- **E410**
- **E450**
- **E550**

Each grade is further offered in **sub-qualities** (commonly labelled with suffixes such as **A / BR / B0 / C**) that differ mainly in weldability and low-temperature impact (toughness) requirements. Only the grade/sub-quality *names* are listed here; the associated numeric property values belong to the copyrighted standard.

## Purpose

To provide a uniform, testable specification for hot-rolled structural steel so that material from different producers has consistent and verifiable strength, ductility, weldability, and toughness — supporting safe structural design and fabrication.

## Relationship to IS 226 (supersession) — search finding

**Finding:** IS 226 is **not** a separately active current standard for new structural-steel design — it has been **withdrawn and superseded by IS 2062**.

- IS 226 (last edition **IS 226:1975**, "Structural Steel — Standard Quality") was the long-standing Indian specification for general-purpose mild structural steel (historically "Fe 410-S" / Grade A type material).
- It was **officially withdrawn**, with IS 2062 taking its place (public references cite the supersession around **2006**, consolidated into the current **IS 2062:2011**).
- Its properties are broadly equivalent to IS 2062 **Grade E250 (Fe 410W)**.
- **Practical guidance:** use **IS 2062** for all new design, procurement, and fabrication. IS 226 remains useful only as a historical reference — e.g., interpreting old drawings, assessing existing/legacy structures, or reviewing archived mill certificates.
